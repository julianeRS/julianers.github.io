About me

My name is Juliane Schwab, I am a PhD candidate in the DFG-funded Research Training Group Computational Cognition at the Institute of Cognitive Science in Osnabr√ºck, Germany. I am supervised by Prof. Dr. Mingya Liu (Humboldt University of Berlin) and Prof. Dr. Jutta L. Mueller (University of Vienna). In my project, I investigate the semantics, pragmatics, and processing of polarity items using psycho- and neurolinguistic methods.

In my research, I aim for an interdisciplinary perspective, combining methods and insights from theoretical linguistics, psycho- and neurolinguistics, and Bayesian statistical modelling to elucidate natural language and its processing in the human mind. I am broadly interested in the semantics/pragmatics interface and the cognitive mechanisms involved in language processing. Currently, my work focuses on processing at the sentence and discourse level.
